Research Projects

Rapid Room-Temperature Synthesis of Metal Nitrides

Aug 2025 – Present

Developing a novel synthesis route for phase-pure nitrides using a modified solid-state metathesis reaction, targeting scalable and energy-efficient production.

Guide: Prof. Ravikumar, Head of Laboratory for High Performance Ceramics, Dept. of Metallurgical and Materials Engineering, IIT Madras, India.

This ongoing project aims to revolutionize the synthesis of metal nitrides, which are critical for advanced electronics and coatings. By re-engineering the solid-state metathesis reaction, we are creating a method that is not only rapid but also operates at room temperature, drastically reducing the energy costs associated with traditional high-temperature methods. My work involves building the custom reaction setup and demonstrating its viability for producing tunable Zirconium Nitride (ZrN). The ultimate goal is to create a scalable, energy-efficient pathway for a wide range of materials, including crucial semiconductors like Gallium Nitride (GaN).

Flexible Ceramic-Based Triboelectric Nanogenerators (TENGs)

Jan 2024 – Present

Pioneered research into flexible ceramic TENGs by synthesizing high-entropy oxides and developing a scalable thick-film fabrication methodology.

Guide: Prof. Ravikumar, Head of Laboratory for High Performance Ceramics, Dept. of Metallurgical and Materials Engineering, IIT Madras, India.

In this project, I addressed a key challenge in wearable electronics: creating durable, high-performance energy harvesters. While ceramics offer excellent triboelectric properties, they are inherently brittle. I overcame this by synthesizing novel high-entropy oxides with tunable properties and integrating them into a flexible electrode structure. A major contribution was developing a scalable thick-film methodology, enabling high-throughput fabrication, a critical step towards commercialization. This work also involved fundamental investigation into the material's flexibility and durability. As a testament to the novelty of this research, I led the establishment of the triboelectricity research facility and methodology within our lab.

Electrocatalytic Green Ammonia Synthesis using LiZnVO4/LiVO3 Nanocomposite

Jun 2024 – Aug 2024

Synthesized novel nanocomposites via a sol-gel route for green ammonia synthesis, achieving 45% Faradaic efficiency and high yield.

Guide: Prof. Sanjay Mathur, Institute of Inorganic Chemistry, University of Cologne, Germany.

During my international research internship, I focused on sustainable energy by developing a high-performance electrocatalyst engineered by incorporating Li for green ammonia production. Using a novel silazane-based sol-gel route, I synthesized LiZnVO4/LiVO3 nanocomposites, individual phase pure compounds LiZnVO4 and LiVO3. This unique biphasic material demonstrated superior catalytic activity compared to its single-phase components, proving a key scientific hypothesis. The catalyst achieved an impressive 45% Faradaic efficiency and a high ammonia yield of 53 µg h-1 mgcat-1, representing a significant advancement in efficient nitrogen reduction in proton-rich environments.

Electrocatalytic Behaviour of Cu-based Nanowires for Nitrate Reduction

Feb 2023 – Jul 2023

Fabricated and studied various Cu-based nanowires for nitrate sensing, achieving enhanced sensitivity and a low detection limit with functionalized versions.

Guide: Prof. Lakshman Neelakantan, Head of Corrosion Engineering and Materials Electrochemistry Lab, Dept. of Metallurgical and Materials Engineering, IIT Madras, India.

This project explored the development of advanced electrochemical sensors for environmental monitoring. I fabricated various copper-based nanowires—including pure Cu, functionalized Cu, and Cu-Pd alloys—within nanoporous templates. By precisely controlling the synthesis via galvanic displacement and DC electrodeposition, I achieved uniform nanowire arrays with high pore filling. Extensive electrochemical testing revealed that the functionalized Cu nanowires exhibited exceptional performance for nitrate sensing, with a high sensitivity of 50 µA/µM and an impressively low detection limit of just 10 nM.

Fabrication of Self-Ordered Tunable Nanoporous AAO Templates

Jul 2022 – Dec 2022

Optimized mild and hard anodization processes to create tunable nano-porous AAO templates with improved scalability and reproducibility.

Guide: Prof. Lakshman Neelakantan, Head of Corrosion Engineering and Materials Electrochemistry Lab, Dept. of Metallurgical and Materials Engineering, IIT Madras, India.

This foundational work was crucial for subsequent nanowire synthesis projects. The goal was to fabricate Anodized Aluminum Oxide (AAO) templates, which act as perfect molds for creating ordered nanostructures. I refined a two-step mild anodization process to achieve precise control over pore diameters, making them tunable from 20 to 80 nm along with barrier layer thinning (BLT). Furthermore, I optimized the more aggressive hard anodization technique, developing parameters that enhanced scalability and reproducibility while critically preventing issues like dielectric breakdown and burning during high-speed growth.

Fabrication and Characterization of Permalloy Thin Films via DC Sputtering

Jan 2024 – Apr 2024

Deposited and characterized Permalloy thin films, analyzing their structural, morphological, and soft magnetic properties.

This project provided hands-on experience with the complete lifecycle of thin-film materials science. I deposited thin films of Permalloy (Fe20Ni80), a material famous for its soft magnetic properties, using DC sputtering. The subsequent characterization was comprehensive: X-ray Diffraction (XRD) was used to confirm the film's structure, Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM) revealed its surface and cross-sectional morphology, and Vibrating Sample Magnetometry (VSM) was employed to plot the M-H hysteresis loop, confirming its characteristic soft magnetic behaviour by extracting key parameters like coercivity.

Synthesis and Characterization of InSb Thin Films via E-beam Evaporation

Jan 2024 – Apr 2024

Synthesized InSb pellets and deposited thin films, confirming stoichiometry and identifying p-type conductivity due to indium vacancies.

Focusing on a key material for infrared detectors, this project involved the synthesis of Indium Antimonide (InSb) from its constituent elements into pellets, followed by thin-film deposition using e-beam evaporation. Structural analysis via XRD confirmed the film's crystallography, while SEM-EDS verified the near-correct stoichiometry. The most interesting finding came from electrical characterization, where Hall effect and four-probe measurements revealed the film had p-type conductivity. This was attributed to the unintentional doping caused by indium vacancies created during the high-vacuum deposition process.

Collaborated in a team to develop a 3D CAD model for a conceptual fruit-picking robot, leading the design of its locomotion system.

As part of the CFI Robotics Club's summer bootcamp, this project was a dive into practical engineering design and teamwork. Our team developed a full 3D CAD model for an autonomous fruit-picking robot from the ground up. My solution was a robust crawler track mechanism, modeled in detail, to ensure stability and mobility in an off-road environment.
