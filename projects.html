Rapid Room-Temperature Synthesis of Metal Nitrides
Developing a novel, energy-efficient synthesis route for phase-pure nitrides.
Aug 2025 – Present

Guide: Prof. Ravikumar, IIT Madras, India.
This ongoing project aims to revolutionize the synthesis of metal nitrides, which are critical for advanced electronics and coatings. By re-engineering the solid-state metathesis reaction, we are creating a method that is not only rapid but also operates at room temperature, drastically reducing the energy costs associated with traditional high-temperature methods. The ultimate goal is to create a scalable, energy-efficient pathway for materials like Gallium Nitride (GaN).

Fabrication and Characterization of Permalloy Thin Films
Hands-on experience with the complete lifecycle of thin-film materials science.
Jan 2024 – Apr 2024

I deposited thin films of Permalloy (Fe20Ni80), a material famous for its soft magnetic properties, using DC sputtering. The subsequent characterization was comprehensive: X-ray Diffraction (XRD) was used to confirm the film's structure, Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM) revealed its morphology, and Vibrating Sample Magnetometry (VSM) was employed to plot the M-H hysteresis loop, confirming its characteristic soft magnetic behaviour.
