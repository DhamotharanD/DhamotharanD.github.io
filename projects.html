Research Projects

Electrocatalytic Green Ammonia Synthesis using LiZnVO₄/LiVO₃ Nanocomposite

Jun 2024 – Aug 2024

Investigated Li-engineered composites for ambient electrochemical nitrogen reduction, overcoming HER limitations and achieving high Faradaic efficiency.

Guide: Prof. Sanjay Mathur, University of Cologne, Germany
  • Synthesized LiZnVO₄/LiVO₃ nanocomposites via silazane-based sol–gel in Stock line with potential-controlled stoichiometry.
  • Studied phase evolution using XRD, FTIR (temperature dependent); structural analysis by XPS, SEM, Raman, TG–DSC.
  • Individually synthesized phase-pure LiZnVO₄ and LiVO₃ for structural property correlations.
  • Overcame HER limitations in NRR via Li-engineered catalyst, achieving 45% Faradaic efficiency and 53 µg h⁻¹ mg⁻¹ yield in proton-rich electrolytes.

Electrocatalytic Behaviour of Cu-based Nanowires for Nitrate Reduction

Feb 2023 – Jul 2023

Synthesized Cu and Cu–Pd nanowires using AAO templates for nitrate sensing, achieving high sensitivity and low detection limits.

Guide: Prof. Lakshman Neelakantan, IIT Madras
  • Synthesized Cu nanowires (78 ± 10 nm) in AAO templates (>90% pore filling) via galvanic displacement.
  • Fabricated Cu–Pd nanowires (CuxPd100−x) via DC electrodeposition with composition control.
  • Compared AAO-templated Cu, Cu₂₀Pd₈₀, and functionalized Cu nanowires via CV/LSV.
  • Functionalized Cu nanowires achieved 50 µA/µM sensitivity and 10 nM detection limit.

Self-Ordered Nanoporous AAO Templates

Jul 2022 – Dec 2022

Designed tunable anodized aluminium oxide (AAO) templates for scalable nanostructure fabrication.

Guide: Prof. Lakshman Neelakantan, IIT Madras
  • Executed mild anodization (45 V DC, 0.3 M oxalic acid) for pore diameters 20–80 nm via two-step process.
  • Optimized hard anodization (150–200 V, <5 °C, current density <500 mA/cm²) to improve scalability and prevent breakdown.

Course Projects

Fabrication and Characterization of Permalloy Thin Films via DC Sputtering

Jan 2024 – Apr 2024

Developed soft magnetic Permalloy thin films and studied their structural and magnetic behaviour.

  • Fabricated Fe₂₀Ni₈₀ films (~316 ± 20 nm) on glass via DC sputtering.
  • XRD confirmed structural properties; SEM examined surface and cross-sections.
  • VSM analysis revealed soft magnetic behaviour with narrow hysteresis loop.
  • Extracted coercivity, saturation magnetization, and retentivity from M–H curves.

Synthesis and Characterization of InSb Thin Films via E-beam Evaporation

Jan 2024 – Apr 2024

Deposited and studied InSb thin films to understand structural growth and conductivity.

  • Deposited ~980 ± 17 nm InSb films by e-beam evaporation; SEM–EDS confirmed In₄₇Sb₅₃ stoichiometry.
  • XRD revealed preferred (111), (022), and (113) orientations with broadening in thin films.
  • Hall and four-probe studies confirmed p-type conductivity due to In vacancies.
